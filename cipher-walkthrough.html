Phase 02 · Lilian Kidston

The discovery call.

Sixty minutes. Lilian uses the intake to build the call — these aren't generic questions. The call surfaces what the form can't capture: the gap between what the CMO believes is happening and what the pipeline data shows.

What this call is for
The form tells me where the numbers break. The call tells me why.
The intake data gives me the pipeline structure, the narrative footprint, and the CMO's own read on the problem. The discovery call is where I pressure-test the hypothesis, probe the gaps, and hear the language the CMO actually uses when they talk about the problem — not what they wrote in the form.
Questions built from the Acme Security intake
  • Your Section 7 read names the handoff problem directly. How long have you known this? What have you tried to fix it?
  • Walk me through what happens between the DMARC content click and the first SDR call. What does the SDR say first?
  • Your win rate is 24% but 42% of contracts are signed by the CFO. What does your champion say to the CFO to justify the spend?
  • What does a lost deal look like in the last 30 days? What did the champion tell you after the deal died?
  • If you could fix one thing in the next 90 days — not everything, just one — what would it be?

Phase 03 · Lilian Kidston

The assessment.

After the discovery call, Lilian documents her observations before the diagnostic runs. This is the interpretation layer — pattern recognition applied to what the intake and the call surfaced together.

Pre-diagnostic assessment · Acme Security, Inc.
Lilian Kidston · Internal
What the intake told me
The 70% consideration drop is the headline number but it's not the whole story. The consideration stage is where the narrative resets — awareness runs on compliance urgency, the first sales conversation runs on product features. The buyer came in responding to one frame and gets pitched something different.
Section 6 is the most important finding in the intake: 42% of contracts signed by CFO, but almost no mid-funnel content exists for that buyer. The champion is believed in the product but has no story to take upstairs. This is a textbook coalition gap.
The DMARC campaign is the proof point hiding in the data. It's the one campaign where narrative and pipeline aligned — compliance urgency frame held from awareness all the way through. That's the template for what the full funnel should look like.
What the call confirmed
The CMO named the pivot point problem herself in Section 7 — she knows the story resets at handoff, she just hasn't had the data to prove it or the mandate to fix it. The discovery call confirmed the SDR script opens with product categories, not threat language. The compliance content earns the click and the SDR script loses it. That's a one-sentence finding with a fixable solution. She also confirmed that champions are losing internal deals because they have no CFO-ready framing — no financial risk translation, no breach cost benchmarks, nothing that speaks the economic buyer's language. Deals aren't dying at the sales conversation. They're dying in the committee.
My hypothesis going in
Primary thread break: Pivot point at consideration. Awareness earns the click with compliance urgency; the sales handoff resets to product features. This is fixable with a homepage rewrite and a 4-email nurture sequence that holds the risk narrative from MQL to SQL. Secondary gap: Coalition gap at decision. Champion has no content for the CFO. A two-page buying committee brief with financial risk framing would close this. Composite NPAI likely in the 4.4–5.0 range. Consideration will score lowest. Decision second. Both addressable within 90 days.
